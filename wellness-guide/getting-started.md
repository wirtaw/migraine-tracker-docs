# 🌱 Getting Started on Your Journey

Taking the first step toward tracking your chronic pain can feel a little overwhelming, but you don't have to do it perfectly. The goal here is simply to observe. Even a small amount of data helps our engine learn your unique baseline!

Here is how to begin your journey with Migraine Tracker.

## Step 1: Access Your Safe Space
Head over to your new wellness dashboard: **[Migraine Tracker Live App](https://migrane-tracker-dashboard.sliplane.app)**

When you arrive, you will be prompted to create a secure account. Because you are logging sensitive health data, your privacy is our top priority. 

To make joining as effortless as possible (especially if you are dealing with a migraine!), we offer secure, one-click sign-in options:
* **Sign in with Google (Gmail)**
* **Sign in with GitHub**

*Note: Using these providers means we never see or store your password, and your personal logs remain strictly encrypted and visible only to you.*

## Step 2: Log Your First "Health Day"
We recommend starting small. You don't need to have a migraine to start using the app! In fact, logging your "good days" is just as important.

1. Navigate to the **Health Logs** section on your dashboard.
2. Gently check in with yourself: *How did you sleep? How much water did you drink? What is your base mood today?*
3. Save your log. That's it! You've successfully started teaching the tracker what your normal baseline looks like.

## Step 3: Recording an Incident
If you do experience a migraine, the **Incidents** tab is there for you.
* Try to log the **Severity** and the **Duration**. 
* If you took any rescue medications, you can log them here too so you can track what works best for your pain over time.
* Don't worry about checking the weather or solar activity—our system will automatically pull that data in the background and attach it to your incident!

## Be Gentle With Yourself
Remember, building a habit takes time. If you miss a few days, simply pick up where you left off. Every entry is a step closer to understanding your body's unique patterns.
