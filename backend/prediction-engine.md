# 🔮 The Prediction Engine

The core value of Migraine Tracker lies in its ability to find hidden correlations between a user's health logs and the physical world. This is achieved through two highly specialized NestJS services within the `src/predictions` module: the **Pattern Guardian** and the **Risk Calculator**.

## 🛡️ The Pattern Guardian (`PatternGuardianService`)

Think of the Pattern Guardian as an asynchronous detective.

**How it works:**

1. **Observation:** Every time a user logs a new `Incident` (a migraine), the Guardian wakes up.
2. **Correlation:** It retrieves the exact environmental data (Weather, Barometric Pressure, Solar Flux, K-Index) for the exact location and time of that incident.
3. **Pattern Matching:** It looks back at the user's historical logs. Did they also have a migraine the last time the Barometric pressure dropped by 5 hPa while the K-Index was above 4?
4. **Rule Creation:** If a statistically significant pattern is found, the Guardian generates a `PredictionRule`.

*Example Rule generated by the Guardian:*
> `IF Pressure < 1010 hPa AND Solar Flux > 150 THEN Risk = 80%`

## 🧮 The Risk Calculator (`RiskCalculatorService`)

While the Pattern Guardian looks at the *past*, the Risk Calculator looks at *today*.

**How it works:**

1. Every morning, or whenever the user loads their dashboard, the Risk Calculator fetches today's environmental forecast for the user's location.
2. It then pulls all the active `PredictionRules` created by the Pattern Guardian for that specific user.
3. It compares today's forecast against the rules.
4. **The Output:** It calculates a final `RiskForecast` payload containing an overall probability percentage (e.g., "High Risk - 75%") and attaches a list of specific triggers that are active today.

## 🔒 Data Privacy in Predictions

It is crucial to note that **rules are completely siloed per user**. The engine does not share rules across accounts. User A's sensitivity to barometric drops will never influence User B's risk calculations.
